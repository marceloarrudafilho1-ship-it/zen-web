Cluster graphs that load in milliseconds.

Force-directed views of who pays whom, computed against a local index of every chain you connect. Pan, zoom, branch, annotate. Nothing is ever uploaded to make it work.

02

P&L on your terms.

Cost-basis modeling that doesn't phone home. FIFO, LIFO, average, or your own rules. Tax-lot exports never leave the disk you choose.

03

Tags & notes that stay yours.

Annotate addresses with context only you can see. Notes live in a SQLite file in ~/.zen. Inspect it, encrypt it, back it up, delete it.

/* the privacy model */

Nothing leaves the device.

Most "private" apps reserve the right to change their mind. Zen can't. There is no server to call.

  • no accounts

    You don't sign in. Zen has no server. There is nothing to sign in to.

  • offline-first indexing

    Plug in a CSV, a local node, or your own RPC URL. Zen indexes once, locally, and never refetches without you asking.

  • your data, your file

    Everything lives in ~/.zen/db.sqlite. Inspect it, encrypt it, copy it onto a USB stick. It's just a file.

  • verifiable, not promised

    The binary ships with a network sandbox. Pull the network cable mid-session. Nothing breaks.
